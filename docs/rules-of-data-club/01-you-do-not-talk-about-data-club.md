But let's break the rules, shall we?

It's 2024. Your fridge claims to be smart. Shops claim to be AI-powered and cashier-free.
Claims. Not facts. Facts are pungent and don't sell just as easily. So then almost nobody wants,
let alone likes, to talk about them.

Enter Data Club 2024.

and time again that openness drives innovation. We see it with Linux. With Signal. With open
source software. With driving a car. As long as prerequisites and constraints are not a blocker,
you drive and drive, and discover, and see, and hear, and think, and meet, and nobody stops and
asks you anything.

It's "us vs them".

üë§ When **DataGovernance** closes a door, it opens another, leading to lower costs, to lower

does not determine the winner, but it does tilt the balance as every second counts in the race.
And to all the nay-sayers, remember it's better to be consistently wrong than constantly
inconsistent.
